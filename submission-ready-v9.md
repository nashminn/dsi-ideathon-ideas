# IDEA 1 — LoanWatch: Loan Portfolio Delinquency Monitor

## Title
LoanWatch — Automated Loan Portfolio Delinquency Monitor

## The Problem
Banks don't lose loans overnight. A missed payment at day 3 becomes a classified loan at day 90 because nobody acted in between. Loan officers managing 200+ accounts can't track each one daily. The data to catch delinquency early exists in every bank's core banking system — what's missing is a system that watches it and acts automatically.

## The Agent Idea
A daily scheduled agent that runs against the bank's repayment data and manages the early warning ladder without any human prompting.

- **DPD 1–7**: Contextual reminder sent to the borrower via WhatsApp or SMS — generated by LLM, referencing their specific loan and payment channel.
- **DPD 7–30**: Loan officer receives a structured account brief — borrower contact, outstanding, payment history, suggested call script.
- **DPD 30–60**: Branch manager gets a consolidated list across all officers. No manual compilation.
- **DPD 90**: Compliance team receives a pre-classification alert with CIB reporting preparation.

Beyond reaction: every week the agent runs a predictive pass over the portfolio, flagging accounts showing early behavioral signals — partial payments, irregular timing — before they miss a payment. Proactive outreach before DPD 1 is the highest-value intervention.

## Who Benefits
Risk and collections heads at banks and NBFIs. Bangladesh has 61 scheduled banks and 35 NBFIs, all under Bangladesh Bank pressure to reduce NPL ratios. Sold as an annual platform license per institution, tiered by portfolio size, with implementation and ongoing maintenance. The same product works identically in Kenya, Nigeria, Pakistan, and the Philippines — NPL is a global banking problem.

## Why Now
Bangladesh Bank has been pushing formal NPL reduction targets since 2023 — this is a regulatory obligation, not just best practice. Core banking data exports are standard at every scheduled bank. LLM-generated outreach is now production-quality and cost-effective at scale.

## Feasibility & Scope
Daily repayment data export → DPD calculation → LLM message generation → WhatsApp/SMS dispatch → weekly predictive scoring → management dashboard. POC: one bank, one loan product, DPD 1–7 reminders only — 3 weeks. No core banking write access required.

# IDEA 2 — HajjReady: Hajj & Umrah Preparation Agent

## Title
HajjReady — Hajj & Umrah Preparation Agent

## The Problem
Bangladesh sends over 100,000 pilgrims to Saudi Arabia for Hajj annually, and more for Umrah year-round. For most families this is a once-in-a-lifetime journey saved for over a decade. The preparation is document-heavy — vaccinations with specific timing windows, medical fitness certificates, passport validity rules, mahram documentation, visa processing. Every year, pilgrims are turned away at the airport for preventable errors. For a family that saved ten years for this, that failure is devastating.

## The Agent Idea
A WhatsApp agent Hajj agencies distribute to every registered pilgrim. The pilgrim registers once — travel date, Hajj or Umrah, passport issue date, travelling with or without mahram — and the agent generates a personalised preparation timeline with conditional logic.

A passport issued within 6 months triggers a Saudi entry advisory. A woman without a mahram follows a different document flow. A pilgrim with a pre-existing condition gets routed to additional medical screening. As each deadline approaches, the agent sends a contextual reminder. At 30, 14, and 7 days before departure it runs a full readiness check. At T-7 it automatically sends each pilgrim a pre-departure document — confirmed vaccinations, baggage rules, emergency contacts — over WhatsApp.

The agency gets a live dashboard: all enrolled pilgrims, completion percentage, automatic risk flag for anyone with departure inside 30 days and a critical item outstanding. Their job becomes exception management, not status chasing.

## Who Benefits
The natural buyer is the Bangladesh Hajj Agents Association — one institutional sale distributes to all member agencies, fitting DSI's enterprise model. Sold as an annual platform license with seasonal knowledge base updates as maintenance. Umrah runs year-round — revenue is continuous, not seasonal. International: Indonesia, Malaysia, Pakistan send even larger pilgrim volumes to the same Saudi system with identical requirements.

## Why Now
Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 Hajj digitisation is raising documentation standards. Bangladesh's Hajj quota has expanded — more first-time pilgrims, higher error rate. No Bangla-language personalised preparation tool exists. The knowledge base is public and updates once per season.

## Feasibility & Scope
Pilgrim registration → conditional timeline generation → automated milestone reminders → completion tracking → agency dashboard → T-7 pre-departure document. POC: Umrah preparation, single agency — 2 weeks. Knowledge base: Ministry of Religious Affairs circulars, Saudi embassy requirements, WHO vaccination rules — all public.

# IDEA 3 — ChronicCare: Chronic Disease Companion Agent

## Title
ChronicCare — Chronic Disease Companion Agent

## The Problem
Bangladesh has 13+ million diabetics. Most see their doctor every 3–6 months. In between, nobody is watching — blood sugar fluctuates, medications are missed, complications develop silently. When the patient finally sees the doctor, the visit is based on how they feel that day, not what actually happened over the past 90 days. Complications that are expensive to treat and devastating to quality of life are often preventable with earlier intervention.

## The Agent Idea
A proactive WhatsApp agent that stays with the chronic disease patient between appointments. The patient logs readings — blood glucose, blood pressure — and symptoms via WhatsApp. The agent monitors patterns continuously: readings trending in the wrong direction, missed medication logs, symptoms that warrant earlier attention. When something needs a doctor before the next scheduled visit, the agent says so and generates a referral-ready summary.

At the scheduled appointment, the doctor receives a structured 90-day log instead of starting from zero. Better data in 15 minutes means better clinical decisions. The agent acts without being prompted — it does not wait for the patient to ask if something is wrong.

## Who Benefits
Three separate institutional buyers for one product: hospitals pay because reduced emergency readmissions improve outcomes and cut cost; pharma companies (Novo Nordisk, AstraZeneca, Sanofi) pay because better medication adherence improves their drug performance data; insurance companies pay because fewer complications mean fewer large claims. Each is an annual platform license — DSI isn't dependent on a single buyer category. Globally: 500M+ diabetics, 1B+ hypertensives.

## Why Now
LLM-based health conversation is now reliable enough for contextual chronic disease check-ins. Manual glucose and blood pressure monitoring is mainstream even in Bangladesh. No Bangla-language chronic disease companion exists. The multi-buyer revenue model means DSI is not dependent on a single procurement decision.

## Feasibility & Scope
Patient logs readings via WhatsApp → agent detects patterns → proactive alert or escalation → 90-day summary generation for doctor visit. POC: diabetes management for a single hospital's outpatient diabetic cohort — 3 weeks. Knowledge base: standard clinical protocols for diabetes and hypertension management. No wearable hardware required — all inputs are manual logs.
